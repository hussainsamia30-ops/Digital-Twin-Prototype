Digital Twin Prototype for Consumer Value Creation in a Smart Living Environment
Project Overview

This repository presents a proof-of-concept digital twin prototype developed as part of a research screening task exploring the application of digital twin technology to consumer value creation, service innovation, and business renewal. The project demonstrates how a physical living environment can be transformed into a navigable digital representation and enhanced with simulated consumer and environmental data to support evidence-based decision-making.

Rather than focusing solely on three-dimensional visualization, the prototype illustrates how digital twins can integrate behavioral and environmental information to generate actionable insights. A rule-based decision layer translates these insights into practical recommendations that enhance personalization, consumer experience, and service value.

Prototype Features
Photorealistic 3D reconstruction of a living environment using Polycam.
Simulated real-time data overlays including temperature, occupancy, smart device activity, engagement, content consumption, comfort, satisfaction, and personalization readiness.
Rule-based decision layer producing actionable recommendations from the observed data.
Research-oriented demonstration linking digital twin technology with consumer value creation and service innovation.
Research Relevance

This prototype reflects the growing role of digital twins within marketing, consumer behavior, and strategic management. It demonstrates how digital representations of physical environments can support personalized service delivery, managerial decision-making, and adaptive business models. Although developed as an early-stage proof of concept, the framework provides a foundation for future research involving IoT integration, machine learning, and empirical evaluation of consumer value creation across B2B and service contexts.
